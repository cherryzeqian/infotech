ABOUT THE LIVING GARDEN

The Living Garden is a small educational website created for kids ages 8–12. It connects real science—about plants, seasons, and city parks—to everyday experiences in gardens, playgrounds, and schoolyards.

Who We Are

Our team worked together to design different parts of the site. Each member focused on a special section but also helped review the whole project so the pages feel like one story.

Member A — Garden Page & Weather Tree

Role: Built the main Garden page and the interactive tree.

  • Designed the homepage so kids can “meet” the tree first.
  • Coded the tree that changes with temperature and seasons.
  • Connected the weather API so kids can see real data from different cities.

Member B — Personal Diary & Navigation

Role: Personal Diary page and shared site layout.

  • Designed the diary so kids can write, save, and revisit their garden notes.
  • Set up local storage so entries stay on the same device.
  • Created the shared header and footer for consistent navigation.

Member C — Hard Facts & Beyond Garden

Role: Garden Fact Lab and NYC Parks explorer.

  • Wrote the Garden Fact Lab content in kid-friendly language.
  • Coded the quizzes so kids can check their understanding.
  • Connected NYC Parks open data for the Beyond Garden page.

How to Use This Site

Kids can start on the Garden page to see how one tree changes with the weather. Then they can visit Hard Facts to learn the science behind what they see.

The Personal Diary lets them record their own observations. Beyond Garden shows how parks across New York City also connect people and nature. Together, these pages encourage young learners to look more closely at the living world around them.

Site credits and references

Project: The Living Garden is a student project for an Information Technology & Web Design course. It is not a commercial site.

Authors: A (Garden & weather tree), B (Personal Diary), C (Hard Facts & Beyond Garden parks).

APIs: Weather data (tree page) and NYC park data (Beyond Garden page) are retrieved from public, free-to-use APIs and NYC Open Data for educational purposes only. No personal data is collected or stored.

Educational sources: Garden science explanations and quiz questions are original writing by the team, based on widely accepted elementary-school science topics about plants, soil, pollinators, and seasons.
